Phase One: Sketching Visualizations

Design Rationale for Sketch 1:

  • RATIONALE_1: Using two distinct (complementary ) colors effectrively separted the populations into the two years in question.
  • RATIONALE_2: I wanted to set the bars horizontally and opposed to each other to allow for size comparison in a given age group. However, The fact the bars are opposite to each other makes it difficult to compare their sizes.
  • RATIONALE_3: Starting at younger ages at the top was a decision that would illustrate how population seems to decay almost steadily with age, inspite of the hundred year difference between both groups. This effect was effective as seen by the inverted pyramid shape formed by the data. I definetly wanted to just show the nature of human life-span: fading as age goes by.

Design Rationale for Sketch 2:

  • RATIONALE_1: Placing the bars on top of each other was done to emphasize the size difference between the age groups which is a lot more effective than in Sketch One.
  • RATIONALE_2: I thought the very distint colors worked well on my First Sketch, so I repeated this design choice.
  • RATIONALE_3: Instead of including a single number to represent the five year bins, I decided to include the bins with the range they represnet labeled. Hopefully this reduces confussion on the average consumer of this graphic who would not have access to my annotations.

Design Rationale for Sketch 3:

  • RATIONALE_1: Though we were warned during the first couple weeks that area does not always convey infomration well, I wanted to try to make square in order to highlight how much larger the male population is in the year 2000 when compared to a hundred years prior. I would say the size difference between the different census years was effectively shown with the aqure shapes.
  • RATIONALE_2: A challenge throughout the design process of visulizations has been to handle all the different age groups. This Visualization definetly seems crowded, so unless it was viewed on some digital media, it would probabbly not be very effective.
  • RATIONALE_3: The legend creation is what communicates the number of people represented by these squares. If this legend is not understood, the whole visual is probably quite unclear. While it was tempting to leave the legend with minimal text, I eflt like a sentence explanation of the legend will aid readers.
  • RATIONALE_4: The colors represent the two different years of census data being presented. Instead of having a legend explaining the color, I used the title and the text color to implicitly indicate which color correspond to which group.

Phase Two: Final Design

Design Process & Design Decisions

The first decison I made was to use Tableau, since it could provide a professional, clean look to my visualization. For the visualization's title, I opted for a font that would give a newspaper look. After several iterations, I decided to use the Toppan Bunkyu Midashi Gothic font. Unlike the protoype visuals, I wanted the final visualization one to communicate the difference in age distribution in the two seprate years, rather than their relative size differences. Meaning the shape of the populations is more important than thier size. Therefore, the two different populations have different axes, wich places more emphasis on the distribution shapes.

The colors for this visulaization were chosen to be de different and easily visible--similar to the sketches. However, unlike the sketches, I picked colors that were less jarring. This goal led me to choos red and peach color which look differnet, but do not draw as much attention as a neon color would. The legend and text explanation were added to the side in order to aid the reader undesrtand the visualizations properly, and pay attention to the two different axes scales. The annotations were placed to the right of the chart in order to let the visualization breath and not feel crowded. Finally, while the raw data simply had numbers to distinguish between age groups, I decided to substitiute the numbers for the ranges they represent. Similar to the first sketch, this vizualization starts with younger age groups on top in order to emphasize the decaying nature of human life-span, regardless of what century we look at.
